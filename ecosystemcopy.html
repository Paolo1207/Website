Ecosystem

Research Title: ECOSYSTEM

Research Content: An ecosystem is a community of living organisms (plants, animals, and microbes) in a specific area. The term 'eco' refers to a part of the earth and 'system' refers to corresponding units. An ecosystem is a community of organisms and its physical environment that interact together. The word ecosystem means ecological systems. Ecology is the study of ecosystems. The Ecosystem are the foundation of the biosphere. An ecosystem can be any size but they are places like a forest or a small pond.

Ecosystems can be classified into three main scales:

- The Micro: a small-scale ecosystem such as a pond, puddle, tree trunk, under a rock, etc.
- The Messo: the medium-scale ecosystem such as a forest or a large lake.
- The Biome: a large collection of ecosystems with similar biotic and abiotic factors such as an entire Rainforest with millions of animals and trees, with many different water bodies running through them.

Ecosystems are often separated by geographical barriers such as deserts, mountains, oceans, lakes, and rivers. As these borders are never rigid, ecosystems tend to blend into each other.
